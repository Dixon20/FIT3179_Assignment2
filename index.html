History of HIV

Many believe that HIV/AIDS originated from Kinshasa, Democratic Republic of Congo around 1920. HIV can be traced back to Simian Immunodeficiency Virus (SIV), which is an virus that attack the immune system of monkeys and apes. Just like COVID-19, the disease that would have slight reaction towards the animal it contracted to might have significantly worse impact in human. Around that time in Congo, people hunted meat as their primary source of foods, and it is believed that SIV jumped to humans when they ate infected chimps, or there were some of the chimp's blood that entered into the cuts or wounds of the hunters. In 1960s, HIV started to spread to Caribbean and Haiti when Haitian professionals brought it to their country upon returning from Congo. The spread was even worsen from rampant sex trade happening at the time, and also from Kinshasa's people migrating across Africa. In 1970s the virus then moved from Caribbean to New York City, and then to San Francisco later in the decade. Then, international travellers to US at the time helped to spread virus even wider across the rest of the globe.

In 1980s there was huge AIDS epidemic in US. In 1981, there were multiple people infected with rare type of Pneumonia. Those group consists of 5 gay men and 1 drug injector. By the end of the year, there were 270 gay men reported with severe immune deficiency and 121 of them had died. This disease was then called as "gay cancer" or "gay plague". Overtime, the disease started to infect heterosexual, children, and 5,000 hemophiliacs at the time, that get infected because they require constant donor of blood. In 1984, researchers finally identified the cause of the disease--the HIV virus--and first commerical blood test for HIV was made available in 1985. In 1988, ART is developed and HIV/AIDS epidemic started to become better because the death rate started to decrease with the help of ART. This epidemic was the one that brought HIV across the globe.

Africa, being the continent where it is originated has the highest HIV/AIDS cases among all continents across all time. When it all started, HIV spread really fast due to lack of education, gender equality, and health care in Africa. Until now it is still an important issue in Africa, but it's not as worse as it was in the past. Overtime, number of new case and death has been reducing in Africa, and also countries around the world due to technological advances. All of this can be observed from the map above.

How does HIV works?

HIV (Human Immunodeficiency Virus) is virus that attacks specific immune system cell called CD4 helper cell or T cell. Not only it destroys CD4 cells, HIV will also uses the cells to make new replica of itself. The decrease in the number of CD4 cell will weaken the body to fight other infections. If left untreated and CD4 counts drop below 200, HIV will turn into AIDS. So for those that wondered why HIV/AIDS is used interchangeably, it's because AIDS is the stage where HIV has become fatal.

HIV can be passed when a person with HIV gets their blood, semen, vaginal fluid, or breast milk inside another person's body. Person's urine, sweat, tears, saliva, or vomit does not posses any risk of HIV unless there is blood in it. There are three stages of HIV. First, the initial infection where the victim experience flu-like symptoms. Second, is the asmpytomatic state(no symptoms) that can last for several years, and that is why people HIV might not even know they have it, so it's important to get tested. Finally, the AIDS stage where victim will experience multiple symptoms such as Pneumonia, rapid weight loss, intense fever, intense diarrhea, blotches on skin, memory loss, extreme tiredness, and usually in this stage people can survive for about 3 years.

Treatment and prevention for HIV aaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaa

How to prevent HIV?

  1. Get tested for HIV regularly if you have active sexual life.
  2. Always, always, and always use condoms every time you have sex!
  3. Do not inject drugs, and if you do never share needle with others.
  4. Choose less risky sexual behaviours such as oral sex. Vaginal and anal sex are the riskiest among all.
  5. Limit the number of your sexual partners.
  6. If you have active sexual life, consider consulting about pre-exposure prophylaxis (PrEP). This is a medicine that need to be consumed everyday and it can reduce risk of getting HIV from sex by about 99%, and from injection of drug by at least 74%.
  7. If you realized that you have done highly risky behaviour. Call your nearest health center, to get post-exposure prophylaxis(PEP). This should be quick because if it is not started within 72 hours of exposure to HIV it's likely that it won't be effective.
  8. Condoms are best used with water-based or silicon-based lubricants to prevent condom break. This is because they do not eat away at the rubber or latex as oil-based lubricants will.

    Miracle drug for HIV

    ART is a mixture of antiviral drugs that are used to treat people with HIV, and it was first introduced in 1980s. Few decades ago, surviving more than 10 years with HIV was considered a miracle. Now, thanks to ART, HIV is no longer a death sentence and people with HIV can live to old age like people without HIV.

    ART works by surpressing amount of HIV virus in the body (Viral load) until it is Undetectable. Individuals with Undetectable viral load can not infect other people through sex, and also towards their unborn child if they are a pregnant mother. That is why beside HIV treatment, it is also effective tool in reducing mother-child-transmission rate. Good news is that most of the area in the world already has adequate ART coverage except Eastern Mediterranean area.

    In Eastern Mediterranean area, ART coverage is still low. People with HIV in that area are heavily stigmatized and discriminated against. Also, in the past decade there have not been initiative to destigmatize HIV. The majority of people in this region also do not have access to HIV testing, and therefore does not know their status. In addion to this, the HIV response is largely dependent on external donor funding due to low priority given to HIV in this area.

    Vulnerable group towards HIV aaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaa

    Which age groups are most at risk from HIV/AIDS?

    Initially the most vulnerable group was under 5 year old. However, as the time goes it becomes 15 to 49 years olds especially younger adults. HIV is primarily transmitted through sex, where unsafe sex is a primary risk factor. Individuals around that age might start to experience with sex, but lack of adequate education regarding sexually-transmitted disease. Another reason might be that around that age, young adult start to try substances and sharing needle without knowing the danger of it. That is why HIV education is really important for young adults in preventing new case.

    Another important age group is Under 5. This age group is prone to HIV because it is possible for mother to infect their unborn child when they are in the womb or as they pass through the birth canal, or through breast feeding. However, a child born to HIV positive mother without any treatment is not always infected with HIV, because only about 25% of babies born to HIV-positive mother without treatment are also infected.

    Is HIV/AIDS more common in men or women?

    The plot shows that HIV prevalence is higher among male in other continents, but not in Africa and Oceania. If we look closely at Africa, there is significantly more female living with HIV than male, and the difference is not little, but very high. This is surprising to see because even though in most continent the number is higher for male, the difference is not as stark as Africa.

    In Africa, there are gender equality and social norm issue that might attribute to higher number of HIV cases among female. In there female have limited role in sexual decision-making, and protection. In addition to that, there are 52 million of girls that have not finished their high school education. Also, due to poverty there are many girls that prostitute themselves. Combining all that factors together make female in Africa highly susceptible to HIV/AIDS. This is why UNAIDS (organisation that fight HIV) uses slogan of "Educate Girls Fight AIDS" in Africa to tackle this issue.

    Law enforcement around HIV issue aaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaa

    Those laws mentioned in the graph can hinder the prevention of HIV. Requiring adolescent to have parent consent before be able to test for HIV would actually prevent them from doing the testing. This is because in some nations HIV has really big stigma around it and that would make the adolescent reluctant to talk with their parent regarding this issue and even worse asking their consent to do the testing. If adolescent wait until legal age, and left untreated, the HIV virus in the body will soon turn into AIDS.

    Even though there are only few, there are still multiple nations that require married women to have their husband's consent to access sexual and repoductive health services. Law like this is detrimental to women and society, because if the husband does not give his consent, the HIV-positive women can infect her husband, infant child (breast feeding), or her unborn child, and this cycle will soon continue.

    Finally, law that criminalise tranmission of, non-disclosure of or exposure to HIV transmission would makes people reluctant to do the testing. This is because if the test result appear to be HIV-positive, the individuals might risk themselves of getting into court. For that reason, most would just choose to remain silent and not get tested, increasing the risk of HIV to spread even further.
